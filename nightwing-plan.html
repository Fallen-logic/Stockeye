BRUTAL TRUTH

⚠ READ THIS BEFORE YOU SKIP TO THE WORKOUTS

Most people at your weight fail within 6 weeks. Not because the program is wrong. Because they underestimate the psychological war of a long cut. 14 kg of fat loss is not a 30-day challenge. It is a fundamental lifestyle restructuring that most people abandon the moment Diwali, a birthday, or a bad week at college arrives.

You will plateau. Around weeks 6–10, your body will adapt to your new caloric intake and fat loss will stall for 1–3 weeks. This is when 90% quit. The correct response is to reduce carbs by 20–30g/day for 2 weeks, not to give up.

You will lose some muscle in a deficit. That's unavoidable. Your goal is to minimize it through high protein + progressive training. Anyone telling you you'll gain muscle while losing fat at this scale is lying to you.

THE BACK PROBLEM — UNFILTERED

THE TRUTH
LATS ON THE FLOOR
The lats are a pulling muscle. They are designed to pull the arm down and back. Every floor exercise is a pushing or isometric exercise. You cannot fully develop your lats without pulling against gravity. Period. Prone Y-T-W and reverse angels build the rhomboids and lower traps, which create the upper V-taper. But the lat width that defines the Nightwing silhouette requires rowing or pull-up variations.
THE HACK
YOUR FLOOR WORKAROUNDS
1. Table rows: A sturdy dining table, lie under it, grab the edge, pull your chest to it. This is a horizontal row. Non-negotiable. Find a table.

2. Bedsheet door anchor: Tie a bedsheet around a door handle, sit on floor, lean back 45°, pull to chest. Primitive but it works.

3. Park visit: One session/week at a park with horizontal bars = lat development. The Nightwing back you want is 80% park bar.

ROUNDED SHOULDER PREVENTION PROTOCOL

⚠
High push-up volume without pulling work = guaranteed rounded shoulders + anterior pelvic tilt. This is the #1 aesthetic mistake beginners make. You will build a thick chest that pushes your shoulders forward. Here's how you prevent it:
  • 1
    2:1 Pull-to-Push Ratio. For every push exercise set, you do two sets of back simulation work. Non-negotiable. This is the structural balance law.
  • 2
    Scapular Push-Ups daily. 3 × 15 every single day, even on rest days. Protract and retract scapulae through full range. This keeps your serratus anterior active and your shoulders in neutral position.
  • 3
    Doorway chest stretch. 60 seconds per side, twice daily. If your pecs are tight from push volume, your shoulders will roll forward regardless of your back work.
  • 4
    Face-down resting posture awareness. When studying or on your phone, check your shoulder position every 20 minutes. Active posture correction during daily life is where most of the battle is won.
  • 5
    Wall angels. Stand against wall, arms in goalpost position, slide overhead maintaining contact. 3 × 15 every push day. This re-educates your upper back to stay retracted.

RECIPES & PRECAUTIONS

⚠ FOOD PRECAUTIONS — READ BEFORE YOU COOK

🚨
These aren't suggestions — they're operational rules. Violating food precautions will stall fat loss, destroy muscle, spike inflammation, or cause digestive failure mid-training.
🔥 COOKING OIL RULES
  • Max 1 tsp oil per meal — ~40–45 kcal. Non-negotiable.
  • Never deep-fry during cut. 1 puri absorbs 8–10 tsp of oil.
  • Use coconut oil spray or dry non-stick pans wherever possible.
  • Mustard oil: use only ½ tsp — pungent, calorie-dense, erucic acid risk at high quantities.
  • Ghee allowed: 1 tsp/day maximum across ALL meals.
  • Avoid refined sunflower/soybean oil — high omega-6 causes inflammation.
🚫 ABSOLUTE NO-GO FOODS
  • Maida: Naan, puri, samosa, kachori, paratha — ban completely during cut.
  • Packaged biscuits & namkeen: Trans-fat bombs disguised as snacks.
  • Sweet lassi/sweetened curd: A mango lassi = 450–600 kcal of sugar.
  • Fruit juices: Even fresh juice spikes insulin without fiber. Eat fruit whole.
  • Aloo in curries: Invisible starch that destroys your carb budget.
  • Dal makhani from restaurants: Cream + butter-soaked — 600+ kcal per bowl.
⚠ MEAL TIMING RULES
  • Pre-workout: 60–90 min before training. Not immediately before.
  • Post-workout: within 45–60 min of training end. Protein + light carbs only.
  • No large meals within 2 hrs of sleep — exception: casein/paneer.
  • Biggest carb meal = lunch. Insulin sensitivity peaks midday.
  • Break fast with protein first — never just chai and bread.
  • Gap between meals: 3–4 hours to let insulin return to baseline.
⚠ PROTEIN PRECAUTIONS
  • Paneer: max 150–200g/day during cut — fat load is real.
  • Eat egg yolks — egg-white-only diets cause biotin deficiency long-term.
  • Chicken: avoid skin and dark meat unless fat is budgeted.
  • Soak dal 6–8 hrs — reduces anti-nutrients and gas significantly.
  • Whey: take with water, not milk — adds 150 kcal and slows absorption.
  • Fish: prefer rohu or surmai — avoid farmed catfish (high contaminants).
💧 HYDRATION RULES
  • Minimum 3.5–4L water/day — high protein requires this for kidneys.
  • 500ml upon waking before anything else. Non-negotiable.
  • Don't drink during meals — dilutes digestive enzymes.
  • Electrolytes on training days — ¼ tsp salt in water if sweating heavily.
  • Coconut water (1 cup) post-workout — natural electrolytes.
  • No cold water right after training — room temp or warm is better.
🌾 CARB PRECAUTIONS
  • Never combine rice + roti in the same meal — double carb load.
  • Cooked rice limit: ¾ cup per meal (~150g cooked = 45g carbs).
  • Roti limit: 2 per meal — never 3+ at a sitting.
  • Avoid carbs at night unless it's a post-workout dinner.
  • Fruit timing: morning or pre-workout only — not as dessert, never at night.
  • Rest-day carb reduction: drop 30–40g. Protein stays constant.
✓ SPICE RULES
  • Turmeric: use liberally — anti-inflammatory, zero calories, free.
  • Black pepper with turmeric always — increases curcumin absorption 2000%.
  • Jeera water on empty stomach: reduces bloating, aids digestion.
  • Avoid packaged masala mixes — hidden sodium, starch, anti-caking agents.
  • Dahi as marinade freely — tenderizes protein, zero calorie impact.
  • Lemon juice everywhere — Vitamin C, iron absorption, zero calories.
✓ PHASE ADJUSTMENTS
  • Phase 1: Cook everything. Avoid raw salads if they cause bloating.
  • Phase 2: Increase protein by 10–15g/day vs Phase 1. Match carbs to training.
  • Phase 3: If weight is stable, add 100–150 kcal on skill training days.
  • Plateau protocol: Drop carbs 20–30g/day for 2 weeks. Never cut protein.
  • High-training days: Add ½ cup rice or 1 extra roti to your biggest meal.
  • Rest days: Drop carbs 30–40g. Protein and fat stay the same.
